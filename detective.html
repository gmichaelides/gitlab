How to Report Phishing

  • For Emails: Most email clients (like Gmail or Outlook) have a "Report Phishing" or "Report Spam" option. Use it! This helps them block similar messages in the future.
  • For Websites: If a link leads to a phishing website, you can report it to Google Safe Browse and the Anti-Phishing Working Group (APWG).
  • For Text Messages: Forward the suspicious text message to the number 7726 (SPAM). This works for most major carriers.

Top 2 Tips for Friends & Family

When explaining phishing, keep it simple and actionable:

  1. "Think Before You Click." Always hover over links to see the real destination. If a message seems urgent or too good to be true, pause and verify it through a different, official channel (e.g., by visiting the official website directly).
  2. "Never Give Out Info from an Unsolicited Message." Legitimate companies will almost never ask for your password, credit card number, or other sensitive details via email or text. If they do, it's a major red flag.
